Holding
REVERSED in part, AFFIRMED in part: • April 2 Statements: Reversed suppression — statements are admissible • April 2 Miranda Card: Affirmed suppression — properly excluded • April 5 Statements: Affirmed suppression — no Miranda warnings given • Motion to Dismiss: Affirmed denial — dismissal not warranted
Rule of Law
1. Exclusionary Rule Nexus Requirement: Evidence is excluded only when obtained "as a result" of official misconduct — there must be a causal relationship between the illegality and the evidence. 2. Attenuation Doctrine: Where the nexus between police misconduct and discovery of challenged evidence is so attenuated as to dissipate the taint, such evidence is admissible. 3. Oral Waiver Sufficiency: A valid oral waiver of Miranda rights is sufficient even if the defendant does not sign a written waiver card. 4. Indictment Dismissal Standard: Dismissal of an indictment requires egregious misconduct AND prejudice to the defendant's rights.
